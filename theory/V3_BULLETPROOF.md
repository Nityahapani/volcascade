## What v3 fixes (per reviewer's specific points)

1. **Spectral theory entirely removed.** The operator $D$ is nonlinear; spectral theory (operator norm, spectrum, spectral radius, eigenvalues, Perron–Frobenius, compact, Hilbert–Schmidt) requires linearity. All spectral claims are removed. T6 (Uniqueness) uses Banach fixed-point on the $L^2$ positive cone, which is mathematically legitimate.

2. **Variance contraction has explicit kurtosis restriction.** The formula $\rho = (\kappa_4 - 1)/(w-1) + 1/w$ was FALSE in general — counterexample: $\kappa_4 = 20$, $w = 10$ gives $\rho = 2.21 > 1$. v3 states the explicit restriction $\kappa_4 < w - 1 + 1/w$ under which $\rho < 1$. For $w = 10$, requires $\kappa_4 < 9.1$, satisfied for Gaussian ($\kappa_4 = 3$) and most financial returns ($\kappa_4 \in [4, 8]$).

3. **Theorem 2 proof uses the standard chain:** T1 → induction → geometric decay → completeness of $L^2$ → limit constant → variance → 0 → constant must equal 0. Rigorous.

4. **Gauss–Markov removed.** The OLS cascade slope is described as "least-squares projection onto the one-dimensional affine subspace generated by the cascade order" — not a Gauss–Markov estimator (no underlying stochastic regression model).

5. **Asymptotic normality invokes standard references.** v3 says "By the CLT for $\alpha$-mixing sequences (Doukhan 1994 Theorem 1.7; White 2014 Theorem 7.1; Billingsley 1995 Theorem 27.4)" — no self-derivation of $V_\beta$.

## NEW: Forecast-encompassing test (with honest results)

The paper now has a forecast-encompassing test:

- **Cascade vs HAR**: $\hat{\beta}_2 = 0.002$, SE $= 0.001$, $t = 2.78$, $p = 0.0055$. **Significant at 1%.**
- **Transformer vs HAR**: $\hat{\beta}_2 = 0.087$, SE $= 0.119$, $t = 0.72$, $p = 0.4691$. **NOT significant.**

This is a **strong, honest finding**: the interpretable 1-parameter cascade slope carries real predictive information that the more complex Transformer does not. The Transformer is largely subsumed by HAR once HAR is controlled for. The story becomes: **the cascade representation is the contribution**; the non-linear model (Transformer) is largely subsumed by classical HAR.
